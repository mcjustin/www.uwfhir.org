Overview

The UW FHIR workshop will be a series of talks and tutorial to explore the healthcare data standard FHIR and two related technologies:  SMART on FHIR, and CDS Hooks.

The meeting will include technical tutorials in the afternoon of Sunday, September 23rd, followed by an all day meeting Monday, September 24th. Most of the day will consist of plenary presentations and panels, with multiple track sessions and an app development showcase in the late afternoon.

Agenda


A specific Agenda is yet to be determined, but will include:

  • FHIR, SMART, and CDS Hooks roadmap/update
  • Managing Innovation using the concept of “EHR as a Platform”
  • Successes with FHIR at innovative healthcare organizations
  • EHR Vendors and FHIR
  • Showcase of Applications
  • Developing a set of SMART on FHIR reference apps
  • Breakout/Interest Group Sessions on:
    • Patient Engagement
    • Security & API Management
    • Software Assurance
    • FDA Regulation
    • Terminology & Modeling
    • Research Data in the Clinical Environment

Goal

Our goal is to bring together those interested in the applied use of FHIR, SMART on FHIR, and CDS Hooks in commercial EHR settings, drawing from our colleagues across the country, but with a particular emphasis on the Pacific Northwest and Seattle. We are engaging those from regional health care organizations, academia, major EHR vendors, our local start-up scene, and professional organizations. These technologies have the potential to significantly advance the “EHR as a platform” concept and maximize both the organizational goals of consistency, security, efficiency, and regulatory compliance with the diverse information needs of individual stakeholders within the organization.
